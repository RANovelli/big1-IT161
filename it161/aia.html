Audience, Issues, and Approach

Client Name/Relationship Chris Jones/Friend, Business Associate
Business/Website Name Screwdriver Bar/Wig Out! 60s R&B, Garage, Soul Dance Party
Redesign/Brand New Site Brand New
Type of Website Marketing, Informational, Community, Entertainment, Social Networking
Client Goals To inform, build community, drive business and increase the following in a reoccurring monthly event at the establishment
Audience Issues, Concerns, Needs Approach
Nightlife Crowd: Individuals in the Greater Seattle Area and beyond who are interested/involved in vibrant nightlife culture. Targeting repeat and new customers alike, from all walks of life, gender-inclusive, 21 and up; the majority between 30-60. All with basic to intermediate computer literacy, engaging from desktop/laptop, some very savvy, definitely all tuned in on their mobile devices, on-the-go, frequently
  • Individuals out and about, looking for a fun evening on the fly
  • Individuals browsing the internet from home or work, anticipating weekend social gatherings
  • Repeat customers looking for information about upcoming events
  • Customers referencing back on media content from previous events
  • A user-friendly smartphone interface with real-time info and navigational links to the establishment
  • A home page with content, images, and links designed to peak interest and drive attendance
  • A calendar with event info, specials, and links to affiliated events at the establishment
  • Revolving content to engage visitors such as updated photos of contest winners, features on guest DJs, short action clips, links to buy merchandise, etc.
Entertainment Industry Professionals: This is a wide net, including business owners, hospitality workers, artists, musicians, music executives, record labels, radio stations, touring acts, their agents, and so on. Similar age range and gender-inclusive as listed above, intermediate to savvy computer literacy, primarily working with laptops and smartphones
  • Local business owners interested in cross-pollenation within the community
  • Touring musicians looking for somewhere to hang out and network while passing through town
  • Radio stations, record labels, artists, etc., wanting to advertise their brand
  • User-friendly, content-rich smartphone interface with easy navigation, location maps, and links to associated community establishments
  • A home page with content, images, and links designed to peak interest and drive attendance, plus general info about the bar, menus, etc.
  • Iconic images, featured songs, and content encouraging like-minded brands to make contact for ad space on the site
Global Subculture Enthusiasts: This is a long-game, word-of-mouth, destination-oriented target audience, focusing on existing online Rock & Roll/Mod communities and associated subcultures represented within this site. Literacy ranging from enthusiast, to blogger, to SOHO DJ, to savvy, multi-faceted user. This audience is also gender-inclusive, where "youth is a state of mind"
  • Individuals recommended by peers who attended while in town
  • Individuals who linked in through affiliated sites, looking for information
  • Individuals who like to stay current and connected within the online subculture
  • A home page with content, images, and links designed to peak interest and engage visitors with the brand, plus general info about the bar, menus, etc.
  • Tons of informative content related to music, style, and the culture this event is centered around. Photos, clips, featured songs, interviews, links to articles, affiliated YouTube and Mixcloud channels, respective social media accounts, etc.
  • Aside from the aforementioned, options to join a mailing list, a contact form, and perhaps a discussion forum to increase site traffic
